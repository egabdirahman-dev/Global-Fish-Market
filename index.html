Executive Summary

Global Market Dynamics

The global fish market is a robust and expanding industry, valued at approximately $1.01 trillion in 2025, with production projected to reach 212 million metric tons by 2034. Aquaculture has emerged as the dominant force, accounting for over 55% of global production.

Post-2020 trends, including the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic, have accelerated the adoption of e-commerce, technological innovations, and a growing emphasis on sustainability and ESG investing.

East Africa's Strategic Position

The East African region is strategically positioned to become a significant player in the global fish market, endowed with vast freshwater and marine resources. Lake Victoria and the extensive Indian Ocean coastline provide a rich foundation for both inland and marine fisheries.

Somalia's Untapped Potential

Somalia represents a compelling case of untapped potential with its 3,333-kilometer coastline and rich marine biodiversity. The country's current fish production is a fraction of its estimated sustainable yield of 500,000 metric tons annually.

Recent regulatory reforms and international partnerships are creating a more enabling environment for sustainable development and investment.

Strategic Recommendations

  • Aquaculture development and technology transfer
  • Investment in processing and value-added products
  • Infrastructure development in cold chain and logistics
  • Public-private partnerships for risk mitigation

Post-2020 Global Market Shifts

The COVID-19 pandemic served as a catalyst for transformation, accelerating technological adoption and sustainability focus across the industry.

Impact of the COVID-19 Pandemic

Supply Chain Disruptions

  • Border closures and reduced air freight capacity hampered fresh seafood exports
  • Restaurant shutdowns created massive demand shock for high-value species
  • Transportation bottlenecks increased post-harvest losses

Industry Adaptations

  • Accelerated adoption of e-commerce and direct-to-consumer sales
  • Increased processing and freezing to convert fresh products
  • Digitalization of seafood trade and supply chain management

Consumer Behavior Shifts

Consumers shifted towards frozen, canned, and processed products due to convenience and longer shelf life requirements during uncertainty. This trend has shown signs of persisting post-pandemic.

Source: Global Fish and Fish Product Market Report

Growing Emphasis on Sustainability

ESG Investing

ESG investing has become a major force, with investors considering long-term environmental and social impacts alongside financial returns. Strong ESG profiles improve access to capital and enhance brand reputation.

Certified Seafood Demand

Common Challenges and Opportunities

Challenges

Illegal, Unreported, and Unregulated (IUU) Fishing

Foreign vessels operating illegally, costing East Africa an estimated $104-260 million annually.

Infrastructure Deficits

Limited cold storage, processing facilities, and poor transportation networks leading to 30-40% post-harvest losses.

Limited Value Addition

Most fish sold in raw form with minimal processing, missing opportunities for higher-value products.

Opportunities

Vast Untapped Resources

Significant potential for increased production from both wild-capture fisheries and aquaculture development.

Regional Integration

EAC market of 180+ million people with harmonized standards and free movement of goods.

Technology Adoption

Solar-powered cold storage, digital traceability, and AI-driven aquaculture systems.
